He has also appeared in several television shows, including The Munsters, The Beverly Hillbillies, and The Dukes of Hazzard. Barris was born in Chicago, Illinois, on November 20, 1925. He began customizing cars at an early age, and by the time he was a teenager, he had already built several custom cars. He moved to California in the 1940s, where he opened a custom car shop. Barris' most famous custom car is the "Batmobile," which he designed for the 1960s television series Batman. He also designed the "Munster Koach" for the television series The Munsters, and the "General Lee" for The Dukes of Hazzard. Barris has been inducted into the Hot Rod Hall of Fame and the Hollywood Stuntman's Hall of Fame. He has also been honored with a star on the Hollywood Walk of Fame. Well known builder of hot rods and custom cars, such as the Batmobile from Batman (1966) and the truck from The Beverly Hillbillies (1962).

George Barris was born in Chicago in the mid twenties. In 1928, he and his older brother Sam moved to Roseville, California with relatives after their parents died. George pursued a passion for building scratch-built aircraft models which led to model cars. The brothers interest in cars intensified during their teenage years as they discovered "the black art" of body work by hanging out after school at local body shops. The movie studios had taken note of Barris kustoms on the streets and at races and came to George for cars for their films.
